# Cinematic Editorial Graphics

Create dramatic, magazine-quality hero images combining photorealistic subjects with elegant editorial typography. Think National Geographic covers, Apple keynote slides, or NYT feature stories.

**Key principle**: Cinematic drama meets editorial elegance. The image tells a story; the typography frames it.

## When to Use

- Blog post hero images
- Newsletter feature headers
- Product announcement graphics
- Milestone/achievement announcements
- Event promotion graphics
- Social media covers (LinkedIn, Twitter)
- Presentation title slides
- Annual report covers
- Case study headers
- Feature story illustrations

Best for **announcements, achievements, and stories that deserve dramatic visual treatment**.

## Quick Reference

| Pattern | Use Case | Key Elements |
|---------|----------|--------------|
| Cosmic/Space | Tech announcements, AI milestones | Planets, stars, nebulae + serif typography |
| Nature Epic | Environmental, scale, journey | Mountains, oceans, landscapes + minimal text |
| Product Hero | Launch announcements | Product floating in dramatic lighting |
| Abstract Tech | AI/ML, data, innovation | Geometric forms, light trails, particles |
| Portrait Editorial | People stories, interviews | Dramatic portrait + overlaid text |
| Architectural | Business, enterprise | Buildings, structures + clean typography |

## Visual Style Guidelines

### Composition

- **Subject dominant**: 60-80% of frame
- **Typography placement**: Top third or bottom third (rule of thirds)
- **Negative space**: Intentional areas for text
- **Depth**: Foreground interest, mid-ground subject, background atmosphere
- **Lighting**: Dramatic, directional, cinematic

### Typography Style

| Element | Style |
|---------|-------|
| Headlines | Serif (Playfair, Times, Georgia) or elegant sans-serif |
| Case | Title case or ALL CAPS for impact |
| Weight | Light to regular (not bold) for elegance |
| Color | White, cream, or subtle color that contrasts with image |
| Alignment | Centered for symmetry, left-aligned for editorial |
| Special | Mix serif + italic for emphasis (e.g., "on *Mars*") |

### Color Palette

| Mood | Background | Accents | Text |
|------|------------|---------|------|
| Cosmic | Deep black, navy | Warm planet tones, star whites | Cream, white |
| Nature | Deep greens, earth tones | Golden hour, misty blues | White, cream |
| Tech | Dark gradients | Neon accents, light trails | White, electric blue |
| Corporate | Deep blue, charcoal | Gold, silver accents | White, gold |
| Warm | Sunset oranges, reds | Golden highlights | White, cream |

## Prompt Templates for AI Image Generation

### For Gemini (Google)

Gemini excels at photorealistic imagery with good understanding of composition and lighting.

#### Base Template
```
Create a cinematic editorial image:

SUBJECT: [Detailed description of main subject]
COMPOSITION: [Framing, rule of thirds, where subject sits]
LIGHTING: [Dramatic, golden hour, rim light, etc.]
MOOD: [Atmospheric, epic, contemplative, etc.]
STYLE: National Geographic cover quality, 8K resolution
SPACE FOR TEXT: Leave [top/bottom] third relatively clean for typography overlay

Technical: Photorealistic, shallow depth of field where appropriate, cinematic color grading
```

#### Space/Cosmic Template (like the Mars example)
```
Create a cinematic space photograph:

SUBJECT: [Planet/celestial body] shown in stunning detail with visible surface features - craters, valleys, atmospheric layers
COMPOSITION: Planet positioned [center-right/center-left], filling approximately 70% of frame
BACKGROUND: Pure black space, minimal stars to keep focus on subject
LIGHTING: Dramatic side lighting creating a visible terminator line (day/night boundary)
QUALITY: NASA/ESA quality photograph, 8K resolution, photorealistic
SPACE FOR TEXT: Clean black space at top and bottom for editorial typography

The image should feel like a National Geographic or NASA official photograph - awe-inspiring and scientifically accurate.
```

## Typography Guidelines (for overlay)

Since AI-generated images shouldn't include text (it often renders poorly), add typography as a post-processing step.

### Recommended Fonts

| Style | Primary | Alternative |
|-------|---------|-------------|
| Heading Sans | GTReallySans, Really Sans Large | system-ui, sans-serif |
| Body Sans | InterVar, Really Sans Small, Inter | system-ui, sans-serif (use letter-spacing: -0.018em) |
| Editorial Serif | Playfair Display | EB Garamond, Cormorant |
| Modern Serif | Freight Display | Tiempos, Domaine |
| Statement Sans | Bebas Neue | Oswald, Anton |

### Typography Hierarchy

```
HEADLINE                          (Serif, light/regular, large)
Subheadline or descriptor         (Sans or serif italic, smaller)
```

Example from Mars image:
```
FOUR HUNDRED
METERS on MARS                    (Serif, caps + italic accent)

The first AI-planned drive        (Serif italic, smaller, bottom)
on another planet.
```

### Text Placement Rules

1. **Rule of thirds**: Place text in top or bottom third
2. **Contrast**: Ensure text area has sufficient contrast (dark bg = light text)
3. **Breathing room**: Don't crowd text against image edges
4. **Hierarchy**: One dominant headline, one supporting line max
5. **Alignment**: Match the formality of the image (centered = formal, left = editorial)

## Post-Processing Workflow

1. **Generate base image** using prompts above
2. **Review composition** - does it have space for text?
3. **Color grade if needed** - enhance contrast, mood
4. **Add typography overlay**:
   - Use Figma, Photoshop, or Canva
   - Apply fonts per guidelines above
   - Ensure sufficient contrast
   - Consider subtle text shadow for readability
5. **Export** at appropriate size for platform

### Recommended Export Sizes

| Platform | Size | Aspect |
|----------|------|--------|
| Blog hero | 1920x1080 | 16:9 |
| OG image | 1200x630 | ~1.9:1 |
| Twitter | 1600x900 | 16:9 |
| LinkedIn | 1200x627 | ~1.9:1 |
| Newsletter | 1200x600 | 2:1 |
| Presentation | 1920x1080 | 16:9 |
| Instagram | 1080x1080 | 1:1 |
| Pinterest | 1000x1500 | 2:3 |

## Quality Checklist

Before finalizing:

- [ ] Subject is dramatically lit and composed
- [ ] Image has clear negative space for text
- [ ] Color palette supports the mood
- [ ] Resolution is sufficient for intended use
- [ ] No AI artifacts or distortions
- [ ] Typography is elegant and readable
- [ ] Text contrast is sufficient
- [ ] Overall composition is balanced
- [ ] Image tells a story even without text

**Red flags to regenerate:**
- Awkward AI-generated text in image
- Distorted proportions or anatomy
- Muddy or flat lighting
- No clear focal point
- Text areas have too much visual noise
- Scientific inaccuracies (for space/nature)

## Platform-Specific Notes

### Gemini Strengths
- Excellent photorealism
- Good at scientific accuracy
- Strong understanding of lighting terms
- Follows composition instructions well

### ChatGPT/DALL-E 3 Strengths
- Very good at following detailed prompts
- Excellent at mood and atmosphere
- Strong product photography capability
- Good at abstract/conceptual imagery

### Tips for Both
- Be specific about what you DON'T want (no text, no watermarks)
- Describe lighting in detail
- Specify where negative space should be
- Reference real-world photography styles
- Request specific resolution/quality
- Iterate with refinements if first generation isn't right
